The evolution of component libraries in 2024

I've been researching how major tech companies are shifting their approach to internal design systems. The move towards headless components styled with utility classes seems to be the dominant trend this year.

At my previous company, we struggled with rigid component libraries that were hard to override. Now, seeing tools like shadcn/ui and various headless UI libraries gaining traction, it feels like we're finally finding the right balance between consistency and flexibility.

What is your team using? Are you sticking with Material/AntD or moving towards custom solutions built on top of unstyled primitives?
